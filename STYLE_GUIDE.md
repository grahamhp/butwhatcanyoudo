## COLOR PALETTE

### Foundation
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Near Black | #111111 | Photography base, backgrounds, gravity |
| Off White | #F5F5F5 | Website backgrounds, text on dark, breathing room |
| Dark Gray | #2B2B2B | Subtle text, captions, metadata |

### Accent Colors — The Three Voices
Each accent carries a distinct psychological register. Use them intentionally.

#### 1. Urgent Red — accusation, emotion, alarm
| Context | Hex | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| On black | #FF4136 | Poster headlines on B&W photography |
| On white | #FF4136 | Website CTAs, pull quotes, key statements |

**Use for:** "The government of Israel told us to." / "Voted yes." / Emotional/accusatory content.

#### 2. Electric Blue — unity, identity, solidarity
| Context | Hex | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| All backgrounds | #00A8E8 | Poster headlines, website unity content |

**Use for:** "Not left. Not right. Just human." / Unity statements / Identity and solidarity content.

#### 3. Vivid Yellow — money, data, evidence
| Context | Hex | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| All backgrounds | #FFC500 | Dollar figures, statistics, data callouts |

**Use for:** "$26 billion to bomb civilians." / AIPAC dollar amounts / Hard data and evidence.
**On-white rule:** Yellow only appears in large, bold headline text on white backgrounds — never in body text or small type, where it would lose readability.

### Color Rule
B&W dominates everything. Color is rare and intentional. Each accent color is used at full vibrancy on every background — no muting, no adaptation. The colors don't hide. When color appears, it should feel like a highlighter on a court document — impossible to ignore.

## TYPOGRAPHY

### Poster Headlines
- **Font:** Oswald or Bebas Neue (Google Fonts, free)
- **Fallback:** Impact, Arial Black
- **Style:** Uppercase, condensed, heavy
- **Letter spacing:** -0.5px to tight
- **Purpose:** Readable from across the street

### Website Body & Subheads
- **Font:** Inter (Google Fonts, free)
- **Fallback:** Source Sans Pro, system-ui
- **Style:** Regular weight (400) for body, Medium (500) for subheads
- **Line height:** 1.5-1.7
- **Purpose:** Clean, modern, trustworthy, high readability

### Citations & Data
- **Font:** Source Code Pro (Google Fonts, free)
- **Fallback:** Courier New, monospace
- **Purpose:** Signals "receipts" — this is sourced, verifiable, real

## POSTER DESIGN RULES

### Design Philosophy — Text First, Image Second
The text IS the poster. Bold, massive, covering a significant portion of the poster's surface. Your eye hits the words first because they are oversized and in vivid color against the B&W background. THEN your brain registers the photograph underneath, and the emotional impact sets in. The words pull you in. The image breaks your heart.

### Layout Structure
Every poster follows this hierarchy:
1. **One bold statement** — DOMINANT. Large enough to read from across the street. Covers 40-60% of the poster surface. Vivid accent color. This is what people see first.
2. **B&W photograph** — the emotional undercurrent beneath/behind the text. Full bleed. Dark gradient overlay to ensure text pops.
3. **One attribution or context line** — white or light gray, smaller
4. **Footer bar** — URL (butwhatcanyoudo.org), hashtag (#ButWhatCanYouDo), QR code

Nothing else. No logos, no extra text, no decorative elements.

### Photography Rules
- Always black and white
- Always real photojournalism — NEVER AI-generated
- Always credited and sourced
- Should be emotionally powerful but not gratuitously graphic
- Overlaid with dark gradient to ensure text readability

### Print Sizes
- **11×17"** (tabloid) — cheap home/office printing
- **18×24"** (standard poster) — print shop standard
- **24×36"** (large format) — maximum impact

### File Formats
- High-res PDF for print
- PNG for digital sharing
- Every poster includes QR code linking to butwhatcanyoudo.org

### Accent Color Assignment by Poster
- **Red posters:** Accusatory/emotional — "They told us to," "Voted yes," genocide references
- **Blue posters:** Unity/identity — "Not left, not right," "Just human," solidarity messages
- **Gold posters:** Money/data — dollar amounts, AIPAC funding, statistical facts

## WEBSITE DESIGN RULES

### General Aesthetic
- Dark-mode-first design (black/near-black background)
- Sections alternate between dark and light backgrounds
- Generous whitespace
- Photography used sparingly but powerfully
- Motion: subtle fade-ins on scroll, nothing flashy

### Homepage
- Opens stark black, white text fading in: "What can you do, anyway?"
- Scroll reveals B&W imagery, then bold color text: "This."
- Three core actions: LEARN → SHARE → VOTE
- District lookup field (enter zip, see your fight)

### Buttons & CTAs
- Primary: Accent red background, white text
- Secondary: Outlined, accent color border, transparent background
- Tertiary: Text link with underline

### Cards (race profiles, poster gallery)
- Dark background (#1A1A1A) with subtle border
- Accent color used sparingly for key data points
- Hover state: subtle lightening

## SOCIAL MEDIA GUIDELINES

### Profile & Banner
- Profile picture: Campaign name in Oswald/Bebas, white on black, square format
- Banner: B&W photograph with campaign name in accent red
- Consistent across all platforms

### Post Templates
- Quote cards: Black background, white text, accent color on key words
- District spotlights: B&W headshot of rep, AIPAC $ amount in gold, "Voted yes" in red
- Sharing format: Always include butwhatcanyoudo.org and #ButWhatCanYouDo

### Tone of Voice
- Quietly powerful, never screaming
- Facts stated so clearly they become unbearable to ignore
- Never preachy, never condescending
- The tone of someone who's done being sad and started being effective

This is forensic. This is evidence. This is accountability presented so clearly that looking away becomes the harder choice.
