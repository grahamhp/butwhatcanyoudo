# But What Can You Do, Anyway?
## A Living Campaign Plan for July 4, 2026 Through November 3, 2026

---

## The Heart Of This

This does not begin as a political project. It begins with two years of grief.

It begins with the feeling of watching, day after day, civilians killed with weapons our country keeps sending. Children. Parents. Whole families. Churches, mosques, schools, hospitals, apartments, streets. It begins with the ache of knowing that the government of Israel is doing this with the backing of the United States government, under both parties, with money and weapons that seem to have no real limit and no real line.

And over and over, when people feel that ache, they are handed the same question:

**But what can you do, anyway?**

It is usually asked as a sedative. A way to make helplessness feel reasonable. A way to make doing nothing feel like the only adult response.

This campaign flips the question.

When someone asks, **But what can you do, anyway?**, the answer is:

**This.**

We can learn who voted yes. We can learn who took money from AIPAC and other pro-government-of-Israel lobbying bodies. We can help elect candidates who will not fund genocide, who will not take that money, and who will not send more weapons to a government killing civilians. We can put the information in front of people in a way they cannot immediately dismiss. We can speak in a new voice. We can refuse the old scripts that make people shut down. We can build something quiet enough to enter the heart and strong enough to move the body into action.

This is for the people who have been carrying sadness in their chests and did not know where to put it.

This is for the people who are horrified but do not feel at home in the language of old protest movements.

This is for the Jewish people who know that what is being done by the government of Israel is not being done for them, even when it is done in their name.

This is for everyone who understands that Jews are not the government of Israel, Jewish people are not AIPAC, and Judaism is not the bombing of civilians.

This is for people across parties, faiths, neighborhoods, and histories who can still recognize one supreme red line:

**No genocide. Not with our money. Not with our silence. Not in our name.**

## The Mission

Build a public, source-backed, emotionally powerful campaign that helps people:

1. Find their congressional district.
2. See what their candidates and representatives have voted for, said, funded, accepted, or refused.
3. Understand the role of AIPAC and other pro-government-of-Israel lobbying bodies in shaping those votes.
4. Share district-specific posters, talking points, and candidate information with people who are persuadable but not already inside activist spaces.
5. Help elect candidates who oppose sending weapons and money to a government committing mass civilian harm and genocide.
6. Make it culturally, morally, and politically impossible to keep funding this without consequences.

The campaign is not pro-Democrat. It is not pro-Republican. It is not anti-Jewish. It is not anti-any people.

It is pro-peace, pro-humanitarian, pro-accountability, and anti-genocide.

The target is not an identity. The target is a set of choices: votes, money, weapons, silence, excuses, and political fear.

## The Voice

The voice must never sound like a normal political campaign.

Not boilerplate. Not activist wallpaper. Not scolding. Not smug. Not clever for cleverness' sake. Not drenched in the slogans people already know how to ignore.

It should feel human. Soft but not weak. Plain but not flat. Poetic but not vague. Quietly loud.

It should feel like someone who has been holding grief for a long time finally found a door.

The language should wake people up without making them feel cornered. It should make them pause. It should let the photo and the words meet in their mind a second later, so the realization lands deeper than a slogan would.

The best lines should feel almost inevitable after you read them:

- Someone tucked them in.
- They called it aid.
- No mother should have to learn the sound.
- Some histories should make us more careful. Not more silent.
- You do not have to hate anyone to say stop.

The campaign is trying to reach the part of people that still knows.

## Non-Negotiable Principles

1. Say **the government of Israel**, not "Israel" alone, when naming state action.
2. Never conflate Jewish people with the government of Israel.
3. Never treat Jewish identity as the problem. Jewish voices of conscience are essential to the moral power of this campaign.
4. Do not use "from the river to the sea" or "free Palestine" as campaign language. Those words carry too much existing fear, confusion, and political hardening for the audience we need to reach.
5. Do not sound like every protest poster people have already learned to walk past.
6. Do not shame ordinary people for having been overwhelmed, afraid, confused, or silent. Invite them into action now.
7. Do not make claims we cannot source.
8. Do not use AI-generated documentary images. Real suffering must be shown with real, verified images.
9. Do not instruct anyone to break the law. Poster and organizing guidance must be practical and legal.
10. Keep the center clear: genocide is the red line that should bring people together across every other divide.

## Strategic Bet

Most people are not moved by being told they are bad. They are moved when something slips past their defenses and lets them see themselves clearly.

This campaign should not try to win the internet argument in the usual way. It should change the emotional frame.

The old frame says:

- This is too complicated.
- This is too far away.
- Both sides are terrible.
- Nothing I do matters.
- If I speak, I will be accused of hating someone.
- But what can you do, anyway?

The new frame says:

- You do not have to hate anyone to say stop.
- You do not have to solve the whole world to stop funding the weapons.
- You do not have to join an old movement to act from conscience.
- You can look up your district.
- You can tell your neighbors.
- You can vote differently.
- You can help make support for this politically costly.

The action must feel concrete enough to begin today.

## What The Site Must Become

The site is not a brochure. It is the center of gravity.

It has to hold the emotion, the evidence, the tools, and the next step. A person should arrive with sadness or anger or helplessness, and leave with something specific to do.

### Homepage

The first screen should carry the original question:

**What can you do, anyway?**

It should not feel like a marketing headline. It should feel like the sentence people have heard in their kitchens, on their phones, at work, from friends, from family, from themselves.

Then the answer should arrive:

**This.**

Not a grand theory. A door.

The homepage should move people into three actions:

1. Find your district.
2. See the record.
3. Share what people need to know.

### Lookup

The lookup page must respect fear and privacy. People visiting a site about a contentious issue may not want to enter their full address first.

Use exactly three modes:

1. **ZIP estimate** - fast, privacy-friendly, approximate. Make clear that ZIP codes can cross congressional district lines.
2. **Enter district** - state plus district number, for people who already know where they are or do not want to share an address.
3. **Use address** - labeled **Most precise**, with the copy: "We do not save your address or anything you enter here."

If a ZIP estimate may be imprecise, say so clearly and offer address or district entry.

### Candidate And Race Pages

The race pages must eventually cover every state and every federal race, but priority starts where the impact is highest:

1. Especially close elections.
2. Redistricted seats where new coalitions are possible.
3. Candidates with heavy AIPAC or other pro-government-of-Israel lobby support.
4. Open seats and challenger races where the public story is still forming.
5. States like Ohio and Arizona, where extra work has already created a model to push outward.

No matter where someone lives, the eventual goal is that they can see:

- Who is running.
- What they have voted for, if they have a voting record.
- What they have said publicly.
- What money they have accepted from AIPAC or other pro-government-of-Israel lobbying bodies.
- Whether they have opposed weapons transfers, called for conditions, called for a ceasefire, or stayed silent.
- Where source links and research notes live.

### Posters

The posters are not decoration. They are the campaign's most portable form of moral interruption.

The graphic designer will replace placeholders, but the strategy is already clear: real black-and-white images, stark colored words, and copy that feels less like a chant and more like a sentence someone cannot forget.

Poster metadata must separate:

- **themes** - what the image or line depicts or references.
- **districtTargets** - where the poster is most likely to work emotionally and politically.
- **careLevel** - normal or high-care.

Recommendations should be based on districtTargets, not just poster content.

High-care historical comparison posters must be used carefully. They can be powerful, but only when the audience and context can hold them.

### Talking Points

The talking points page should explain why the campaign uses new language.

The point is not to abandon principle. The point is to reach people who might agree in their conscience but shut down when they hear familiar political signals.

The page should make room for people who are horrified by civilian suffering, opposed to genocide, and still afraid of being misunderstood as anti-Jewish or pro-violence. The campaign should give them a morally clear place to stand.

### How-To And Organizing

The how-to page should be practical, calm, and legal.

It should show people how to print posters, ask businesses for permission, share materials, talk to neighbors, form local groups, and show up at public political events without crossing legal lines.

It should make action feel possible for people who have never been organizers.

## Data And Research Approach

The site only has power if people can trust it.

All data, snapshots, research notes, candidate notes, and source-backed public datasets should live in GitHub with the project.

There should be **no nightly sync**. A nightly process is one more failure point, and this campaign does not need fake freshness. The public information available now is enough to begin. Updates should be deliberate manual runs, reviewed before commit.

Use a mix of:

- Official House Clerk roll call XML files.
- FEC filings.
- OpenSecrets and other campaign finance sources.
- Track AIPAC and similar funding/endorsement trackers.
- Ballotpedia, Cook Political Report, state election sites, and reliable local reporting.
- Official candidate websites.
- Candidate interviews, social posts, press releases, and public statements.
- APIs only where they reduce error and can be checked against saved source snapshots.

For candidates with voting records, the voting record is the backbone.

For candidates without voting records, research must be individual and source-backed. Their websites, public statements, social posts, donors, endorsements, and funding relationships matter. If their stance is unknown, say unknown. Do not fill silence with certainty.

Ohio and Arizona are the proof-of-concept states. The structure built there should be pushed to all other identified states, then expanded until every state has useful candidate information.

## Candidate Database Fields

Each candidate or race should be tracked with enough structure to power pages, lookup results, posters, and fact-checking.

Core fields:

- State and district.
- Office and election year.
- Incumbent, challenger, open seat, or primary candidate.
- Party, but never as the main moral frame.
- Race rating and competitiveness.
- Redistricting status.
- AIPAC funding, endorsements, and source links.
- Other pro-government-of-Israel lobbying support, where sourceable.
- Key votes on weapons transfers, military funding, ceasefire, conditions, and related bills.
- Public quotes or statements.
- Candidate stance summary with confidence level.
- Source notes and date checked.
- District messaging notes.
- Recommended poster targets.

The database should be humble where the evidence is incomplete and direct where the evidence is clear.

## Current Priority Map

Priority is not the same as moral importance. Every district matters. But time is limited, so the first push should focus where action can move seats or shape the narrative.

### Highest Priority

- Close federal races.
- Redistricted districts.
- Open seats.
- Heavy AIPAC or pro-government-of-Israel lobby involvement.
- Districts where a credible anti-genocide candidate is running.
- Districts where a candidate without a voting record needs individual research.

### Proof-Of-Concept States

- Ohio.
- Arizona.

These states should model the structure for every other state: district context, candidate records, source links, tailored messaging, and poster recommendations.

### Long-Term Goal

Every state. Every relevant federal race. Every voter able to see what their candidates have done or said.

## Poster Direction

The campaign should add, not replace, poster concepts as the voice sharpens.

Current direction includes:

- Someone tucked them in. / Someone voted yes.
- They called it aid. / It arrived as metal.
- No mother should have to learn the sound. / Not there. Not anywhere.
- Some histories should make us more careful. / Not more silent.
- The old photographs warned us. / The new ones are asking us.
- Never again was not meant for one people only.
- You do not have to hate anyone to say stop. / But you do have to mean it.

The best posters should not tell people what to think in a loud voice. They should create the moment where people realize what they already think.

No Spanish posters for now. Add language expansion later when the core voice is right and translation can be done with care, not by flattening the work into literal versions.

- Add source links for every vote, quote, funding claim, and endorsement.

Make the lookup flow feel trustworthy, private, and useful.

Immediate needs:

- Improve ZIP ambiguity messaging.

Turn the placeholder gallery into a serious visual campaign.

Immediate needs:

- Build district-targeted poster sets.

Give people a new way to talk about this.

Immediate needs:

- Create language for parents, faith communities, veterans, students, fiscal conservatives, Jewish audiences, and broad persuadable voters.
- Keep every version pro-peace, pro-humanitarian, anti-bigotry, and anti-genocide.

### 5. Volunteer And Local Organizing

Make action feel possible for normal people.

Immediate needs:

- Volunteer form.
- Local group starter guide.
- Print shop outreach template.
- Business poster permission template.
- Legal display guidance.

The campaign should be shareable because it feels different.

Immediate needs:

- Launch video explaining the origin of the question.
- Poster reveal posts that give the words room to land.
- District spotlight posts with source-backed facts.
- Creator outreach to people who already care but need a concrete thing to point toward.
- Encourage remixing while protecting the campaign's red lines.

## Risk And Care

### Antisemitism Accusations

The campaign must be morally clear and careful at the same time. Always separate Jewish people from the government of Israel. Feature Jewish voices. Say directly that this is not against Jews. Do not let anyone use the campaign as cover for hate.

### Accuracy

Every factual claim needs a source. Every number needs a link. Every quote needs context. If something is uncertain, mark it uncertain.

### Legal Safety

Do not tell people to post illegally. Give practical, legal ways to display materials and ask permission.

### Emotional Safety

The imagery will be heavy. The campaign should not exploit suffering or turn grief into aesthetics. Use real images with care, dignity, and purpose.

### Burnout

This began personally, but it cannot stay only personal. The plan must make room for volunteers quickly, so the work can spread without crushing the person who started it.
