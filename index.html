It's already happening

When voters say enough, things change.

Thomas Massie, a Republican congressman from Kentucky, started questioning whether the U.S. should keep sending weapons to the government of Israel as the civilian death toll mounted. His own party primaried him out.

In New York, Zohran Mamdani — a Democrat — ran against a well-funded establishment candidate backed by millions in lobby money. Voters chose him anyway.

Both parties punish dissent on this issue. That's why this isn't about parties. It's about people. And when people come together, they win.

Not left. Not right.
Just human.

This campaign is not pro-Democrat or pro-Republican. It's not pro-any-party. It is for every American who believes that their tax dollars should not fund the killing of civilians — and who wants to do something about it.

If there's one issue that can bring us together, saying no to genocide should be it.
