It's already happening

Both parties tried to silence dissent on this issue. In both cases, voters pushed back.

Thomas Massie, a Republican congressman from Kentucky, started voting against weapons packages to the government of Israel as the civilian death toll climbed. The Republican establishment spent heavily to primary him out — removing one of their own for breaking ranks.

In New York, the Democratic establishment did the same thing — pouring millions into a candidate to stop Zohran Mamdani, a Democrat who opposed the weapons packages. Voters chose Mamdani anyway.

The establishment will fight back. But this issue isn't going away. And when enough people show up, they win.

Not left. Not right.
Just human.

This campaign is not pro-Democrat or pro-Republican. It's not pro-any-party. Your representative might agree with you on the economy, on healthcare, on taxes. But if they also vote to send weapons used to kill tens of thousands of civilians — we think that automatically disqualifies them.

Once we show every politician in America that voters have red lines they will not cross, they can come back and earn our votes on everything else.
