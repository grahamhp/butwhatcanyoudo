About

The origin story. Why this exists and who's behind it.

"But what can you do, anyway?"

Everyone kept saying it. At dinner tables, in group chats, in the comments. People watched the news and felt sick. They knew something was deeply wrong. But every conversation ended the same way:

"What can you even do about it?"

This site won't pretend to have all the answers. But it's one attempt to stop saying nothing — and to give people a few real, concrete things they can do. Small things, maybe. But together, they might just matter.

The idea

A lot of people have watched what's happening and felt something break inside them. But the conversation about it has been stuck for a long time — trapped in language and framing that most people either don't recognize or actively tune out.

The idea behind this campaign is simple: talk about it differently. Not louder. Not angrier. Just more clearly, and in a way that meets people where they actually are — not where activists wish they were.

The facts are all public record. The foreign lobbying money, the votes, the weapons packages, the civilian death toll. What's been missing is a way to put those facts in front of ordinary voters and connect them to something they can actually do about it.

That's the idea. Make it visible. Make it local. Make it about November.

The approach

Nonpartisan

This is not a left or right campaign. Both parties take the money. Both parties cast the votes. We hold all of them accountable equally.

Fact-based

Every claim is backed by public data: FEC filings, congressional vote records, and official sources. We don't deal in rumors or speculation.

Action-oriented

We don't just raise awareness. We give people specific things to do: look up their rep, print a poster, talk to their neighbors, and vote in November.

Grassroots

No PAC money. No corporate sponsors. No billionaire donors. This campaign runs on people power and the truth.

Who's behind this

This campaign was started by two people — who kept watching the news, kept feeling sick, and kept hearing the same question from everyone around them:

"But what can you do, anyway?"

For a long time, the honest answer was: We don't know. We couldn't answer it. We didn't have a plan. We just knew that when our kids ask us someday what we did during all of this, we didn't want to tell them nothing.

So we built this. Not because we have all the answers. Not because we're experts in politics or campaigning or organizing. But because someone had to try, and we figured it might as well be us.

The idea is simple: take the facts that are already public — the money, the votes, the death toll — and make them impossible to ignore. Put them on posters. Put them on a website. Put them in front of voters in the districts where their vote can actually change things. And do it all in language that doesn't alienate the people we need to reach.

This isn't a PAC. This isn't an organization. This is two people with laptops, some design tools, and a question that won't go away. If it grows — and I hope it does — it'll grow because people like you decided to do something too.

— Graham & Silvia

How this is different

There are a lot of campaigns and organizations working on this issue. Many of them do important work. But this one is built on a different set of assumptions about how to actually change minds.

We don't use protest language

No slogans, no chants, no language that makes people feel like they have to pick a side in a conflict they don't fully understand. We use plain English and public data.

We target the persuadable, not the convinced

Most campaigns speak to people who already agree. We're focused on the vast middle — people who are uncomfortable with what's happening but haven't been given a way in that doesn't feel radical or scary.

We focus on the vote, not the protest

Protests matter. But the thing that actually removes someone from power is a ballot. Everything on this site points to one goal: changing who sits in Congress after November 2026.

We welcome everyone

Jewish, Muslim, Christian, atheist. Republican, Democrat, independent. If you believe your tax dollars shouldn't fund the killing of civilians, you belong here. Full stop.

Transparency

This campaign has no dark money, no hidden agenda, and no institutional backing. Here's exactly how it works:

✓

Funding: Zero. This is volunteer labor and personal time. If that changes, it will be stated openly.

✓

Data sources: FEC filings, congressional vote records (congress.gov), Ballotpedia, Cook Political Report. Every claim is cited.

✓

Photos: All images are real photojournalism from credited sources. Nothing is AI-generated. Ever.

✓

Corrections: If we get something wrong, we fix it. If you spot an error, tell us.
